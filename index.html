Visibility Plan

You don't need to post more. You need to post in a way that feels like you and actually reaches her. This is built for how you're wired. Your story is your strongest pull, so we lead with your real experience, not polished tips. Start with the 7 days below to build your tolerance for being seen, then settle into the weekly rhythm. Tap any day to open it.

Before you start

Visibility is a nervous system skill, not a personality trait. Every time you let yourself be seen and nothing bad happens, your body learns it's safe. That's the whole game. Small and steady beats big and rare. If a day feels like too much, do the smaller version and still count it as a win.

✦Your 7-day on-ramp
1
Show your face, no words needed
Day 1

Post one story. Just you. In your studio, out in nature, mid-paint, or at your desk. No caption pressure, no teaching. A few words over the top if you want: "Morning in the studio." That's it.

The goal today isn't engagement. It's letting your body feel what it's like to be visible and have it be completely fine.

2
Name one thing you believe
Day 2

One short post or story. A single thing you know to be true from your work. Something like: "The most self-aware people I work with are often the most stuck. Knowing why you do something lives in a different place than changing it."

No call to action. No selling. You're just letting people hear how you think. This is how your soulmate client starts to recognize you.

3
Share a piece of your story
Day 3

This is often your strongest move. You're a 3/6, and you're in the middle of a real transition, so your story carries weight other people's tips never will. Share one honest moment from your own path. The version you don't usually say out loud.

Maybe the day you realized the weekly session model was quietly draining you, even though you love the work. Keep it real, not polished. People come for the truth, not the highlight reel.

4
Mirror the person you help
Day 4

Write directly to her. The deep-feeling woman who has done years of therapy, understands her patterns, and still keeps repeating them. Name what that's like so precisely she feels seen.

Something like: "You've had the same conversation with your partner more times than you can count. You both know your parts. Nothing changes. That isn't a failure of insight. It's a sign the work needs to go somewhere talk can't reach." Let her feel that you get it.

5
Show your work in motion
Day 5

A glimpse behind the scenes. Your art materials, a piece in progress, the space you work in, a walk in nature where a session happens. A short note on what one part of your method actually does, in plain language. Why a brush can reach what words can't.

You're not explaining your whole method. You're letting people feel the texture of working with you.

6
Open a small door
Day 6

First soft invitation. No big launch. Just let people know there's a way in. Once your assessment call is live, this becomes: "If something here is landing, I have a paid assessment call where we look at what's actually going on for you and whether we're a fit. Comment or message me and I'll send the details."

Until that's ready, point them to your current way of working or simply invite a conversation in your messages.

7
Rest and notice
Day 7

No posting today. Instead, notice. What felt easier than you expected? Which post felt the most like you? What did your body do when you hit publish, and did it settle?

Your design works through response, not forced production. This is the day you let the week land and pay attention to what your body is telling you about what's worth doing again.

When Fear Hits

For the moments it gets loud. Before you post, before you hit publish, when the old "who am I to do this" voice shows up. None of this means something is wrong. It means you're growing past an old version of yourself, and your body is catching up. Use whichever one fits the moment.

Safe to Be Seen
Hypnosis · find somewhere quiet, headphones on

When the fear of being seen feels bigger than a tapping round can hold, put your headphones on and let this take you under. Lie back, close your eyes, and let your nervous system settle into safety. Don't listen while driving.

✦Out of Flight-or-Fight Breathwork

Do this before you post, before a call, before you open something that makes you nervous. It takes your nervous system out of fight-or-flight so you're working from calm, not panic.

  • Both feet flat on the floor. Feel them. Press down gently.
  • One hand on your heart, one on your belly. Let them rest there.
  • Breathe in through your nose for 4, hold for 4, then out through your mouth for 6, lips soft like you're breathing through a straw. The long exhale is what signals safe. Do this four times.
  • Say quietly: "I'm safe right now. Nothing here is an emergency."
  • Drop your shoulders. Unclench your jaw. Now go do the thing.
Ready

Four breaths. In through your nose, hold, out through your mouth like a straw. Follow the circle.

✦EFT Tapping

EFT (tapping) calms the part of your brain that fires when you feel exposed. You tap gently with two fingers on each point while saying the words out loud. Say them even if you don't fully believe them yet. The point isn't to lie to yourself. It's to let your body hear the truth alongside the fear.

Tap each point 5 to 7 times as you say the line, moving through them in order. Each script has two rounds. The first round lets the fear speak, the second round turns it toward what's true. Notice where your fear sits on a scale of 1 to 10 before you start, then check again after both rounds.

Open the one you need

Read the title of each script, notice which fear is loudest for you right now, and tap that one open. You don't have to do them all. Start with the one that matches what you're feeling, and do both rounds.
